HIMACHAL PRADESH

The Word Himachal means "Snow covered Peaks".

Himachal Pradesh is a state in the western Himalayas of India. It is known as the "Mountain State" and is characterized by its extreme landscape, which includes several peaks and extensive river systems. The state has a population of 68,56,509 and an area of 55,673 sq. km. The capital of Himachal Pradesh is Shimla, and the main languages spoken in the state are Hindi and Pahari.

Himachal Pradesh is known for its rich culture and traditions, and is dotted with stone and wood temples. The state is also known as "Dev Bhumi" and is believed to be the abode of gods and goddesses.


Some of the top destinations in Himachal Pradesh include:

  • Shimla
  • Dharamshala
  • Manali
  • Chamba
  • Spiti
  • Valley
  • Great Himalayan National Park.

Tourism in Himachal

The best time to visit Himachal Pradesh is February to June (Spring and Summer). The winters, i.e., between October and February when it's the snowfall season, is also a popular time to visit.

Himachal Pradesh boasts a myriad of tourist attractions that cater to all interests. Shimla, the state capital, is renowned for its colonial architecture, Mall Road, and the scenic toy train ride on the Kalka-Shimla Railway. Manali, nestled in the Kullu Valley, attracts adventure seekers with its skiing slopes, paragliding spots, and the mystical Rohtang Pass.
