Haryana: Where Tradition Meets Modernity in the Heart of India

Haryana is a North Indian state surrounding New Delhi on 3 sides. The Yamuna River runs along its eastern border with Uttar Pradesh. Shared with Punjab, the state capital Chandigarh is known for its modernist buildings and gridlike street plan designed by Swiss architect, Le Corbusier. The Zakir Hussain Rose Garden features 1,600 species, while its Rock Garden showcases sculptures made with recycled materials.

Tourism in Haryana

Haryana may be small in size, but it packs a punch when it comes to tourism offerings. From ancient heritage sites to modern attractions, the state invites travelers to explore its diverse landscapes, immerse themselves in its rich culture, and create lasting memories amidst its myriad attractions.

Haryana, often overshadowed by its neighboring states, offers a treasure trove of tourist attractions that showcase its rich history, vibrant culture, and natural beauty. From ancient historical sites to modern marvels, Haryana provides a diverse range of experiences for travelers to explore.
